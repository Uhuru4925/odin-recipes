Italian sunday sauce

Description

What is Italian sunday sauce

Italian Sunday sauce, also known as "gravy" in some Italian-American communities, is a rich and hearty tomato-based sauce that is traditionally prepared on Sundays. It is typically made with a combination of tomatoes, garlic, onions, herbs, and various types of meat such as meatballs, sausage, and pork ribs. The sauce is simmered for several hours to develop deep flavors and is often served over pasta.

How to serve Italian sunday sauce

Italian Sunday sauce is typically served over cooked pasta, such as spaghetti or rigatoni. It can also be used as a base for other dishes like lasagna or baked ziti. The sauce is often accompanied by grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil or parsley for added flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound Italian sausage (mild or spicy), casings removed
  • 1 pound ground beef or pork
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (15-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 (6-ounce) can tomato paste
  • 1 cup beef broth or red wine
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  2. Add the Italian sausage and ground beef or pork to the pot. Cook until browned, breaking up the meat with a spoon as it cooks. Drain any excess fat if necessary.
  3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, and beef broth or red wine. Mix well to combine.
  4. Add the dried basil, dried oregano, red pepper flakes (if using), salt, and pepper. Stir to combine all the ingredients.
  5. Bring the sauce to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the sauce simmer for at least 1 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally. The longer the sauce simmers, the more the flavors will develop.
  6. Serve the Italian Sunday sauce over cooked pasta, garnished with fresh basil or parsley and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
